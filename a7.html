A7: Project Proposal

Project Description

When I travel, I often want to bring one, two, or even three types of loose-leaf tea and filter bags to brew a good cup of tea. I don’t currently have a good set up, so I end up carrying around bags of loose-leaf tea and ziplocks with filters in them. For my final project, I designed a set of small, modular 3D-printed tea storage caddies that snap together and snap to a small box to store filters.

Design

Below are some preliminary sketches. I identified the parts of the system where I needed to make design decisions, and worked out pros and cons through sketching – for example, deciding to make sliding lids instead of hinged lids to reduce complexity.

Drawer Shape

For the drawer, I decided I'd rather have the openings on the "top" instead of sliding drawers, so I can worry less about latching mechanisms and drawers "sliding out"

Lid Mechanism

Designing hinges seemed like a project in itself, so I decided to not overcomplicate and use a slide-in "lid" mechanism

Connectability and Modularity

I thought about various press-fit options for attaching the tea boxes together, but I want to first try embedding magnets in the print to be able to connect modules without parts that can wear and tear.

Lid slide style

I tried to figure out if there was a pro to a thicker lid with an H-shape on a track, rather than a thinner lid, but my woodworking husband reassured me the simpler, thinner version is best.

Filter Storage

The filter storage should snap to the bottom of 2 tea boxes pressed together, with the same sliding lid mechanism.
