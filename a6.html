Making the Mold

To create my silicone mold, I made a simple box with one removable side so I could easily pry the silicone off the mold. For the first half, I use the silicone I originally ordered, which was Smooth-Sil 940 (see data sheets below). It's pink!

Casting time!

For my first cast, I used a very precarious setup to put pressure on my mold. The result was just ok! I forgot to take a picture, but plaster leaked between the molds and made ea "raft" around the coaster. I was able to break it off easily but I adjustd my plans for the second cast, where I used clamps and cut a custom piece of wood for a more even and secure fit.

Note: Before I made my first cast, I filled my mold up with water and measured the interior volume using a scale so I could avoid mixing way too much or way too little plaster. This method worked pretty well and I barely had any waste!
