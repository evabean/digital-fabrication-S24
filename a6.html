The TLDR of this project is: Don’t print circles! I tried so hard to make a mesh screen for my lamp with circular holes, and eventually I decided I either had to smash my 3d printer to pieces of choose a different direction (I chose a different direction because I want to go to grad school!)

Designing An In/Organic Lamp That Might Work

When gathering ideas, I was mostly drawn to lamps that had mesh screens to let light through. My bed wasn’t big enough to do the “foldover” method I originally wanted to do, so I designed a version with two smaller screens sandwiching the bulb.

I started with a round base and a square mesh dot screen, but when I designed my attachment points (press-fit buttons on the front and back face), I realized that I would either have to wrap the mesh around the lamp (see image below) and calculate the button alignment, or have only one attachment point which seemed sketchy. I printed a few test cubes to figure out the button fit tolerance, and then there was an interlude where I printed half a round base, realized this was going to be a problem, then smashed it to bits trying to get it off the glass plate.

Circle Hell

All my problems started with circles. When I printed my first test mesh, I had lots of issues getting the circles to stick but when cleaned the bed with Dawn soap and acetone, slowed down to 40%, and increased nozzle temperature to 212, I made it work. But when I went to print the larger screen, I could not get the circles to work. I restarted the print almost 30 times because one little circle would fly off, or bunch up. I tried the glass bed, the metal bed, the metal bed with hairspray, and nothing worked. Things were also sticking too much to the glass bed. I tried re-leveling the bed several times. At one point I got about 15% in to printing and then, for reasons unknown to me, the front right corner appears to have become misaligned and the nozzle started dragging through previous print material. I set myself a deadline for 5pm and said I would design something else if I didn’t make it work and reader, I did not make it work.

Pivoting to a more Linear Hell

don’t like this lamp concept as much, but I switched to a line mesh pattern because I knew it would be easier to print. At this point, it’s Monday afternoon so I cannot dilly dally and I must do what I know will go smoothly. And it did! Luckily my rhino skills are getting stronger so it was easy for me to put together a linear screen. I had a few misprints but overall it went much better than the circle concept. I will never draw a circle again, I am a line girl forever.

Lessons Learned

  • Your printer has to be VERY well calibrated to make hundreds of tiny circles! I had neither the time nor patience to calibrate it.
  • The glass bed is too adhesive, or at very least, I need to experiment with placing the nozzle further from the bed than I would with the metal one. Only one glass bed was sacrificed this week and hopefully that protects me from further curses.

What about molds?

I know you're going to be disappointed, Prof Nadya, but I have not started casting yet. I have designed a mold, and at the time of writing this I am attempting to print the first half (with a raft to avoid any adhesion isssues!). I didn't do a ton of planning or ideating – I picked a simple shape clean edges to make this as easy as possible for myself, and I made a practical item because I'm a practical person and I couldn't stand to all this work and not have an object to admire from it.
