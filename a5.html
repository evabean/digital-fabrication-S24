Hello Kitty and the Bad Mesh

I unfortunately missed class last week, and to make sure I made it thorugh this assignment, I picked something that I hoped would have a slightly less complex mesh than the examples I saw in the discord chat. My Hello Kitty obsessed co-worker just moved and has a new desk setup, so I thought I'd make her a little trinket box with her favorite character on top as "handle."

Preparing the Hello Kitty box file

Preparing the Hello Kitty box file

But making a good mesh is hard. I staretd by just algining Hello Kitty's bum with the top of the box, but when I ran RepairMesh, the result was bad.

Not a good mesh

I thought it might help to bring Hello Kitty deeper into the box lid, to create a connection with less overhang. The results were better, but not fantastic aka still Bad Mesh.

STILL not a good mesh

To me, 2 pairs of intersecting faces seemed like it could work. Again, I was not in class, so how could I know? I decided to experiment by also slicing and printing a reliable shape (orb) and see if the results were diffeent between the two.

Orb with the good mesh

Lining up my little trinkets in Cura

Unfortunately for Prof Nadya, I received some cute filament in the mail this week so I decided to print in purple. I had no issues swithcing filament.!

Cute filament!

The first version was a little warped but had no issues slicing or printing related to mesh boolean operations. Satisfied, I switched from my metal bed to a glass bed and experienced EXTREME ADHESION. I also turend the settings from "low quality" to "standard."

First verison, with supports

First verison, with supports. Bed adhesion issues caused warping on the boxes but no issues with the merged parts of the mesh.

First version on the right, second version on the left. For the second version, I used a glass bed and switched from "low quality" to "standard quality" in Cura.
