A8: Final Project

Overview

My plan for this project was to make a set of modular, magnetically connected boxes to carry small amounts of loose-leaf tea and filters bags to brew it. The plan was to use combination of parametric modeling, 3d printing, stock parts, and friction fit, with contingency plan to omit stock parts if necessary. In the end, I managed to fulfill almost all of my plans and didn’t sacrifice any “features”, only an extra box print, to make it work.

Reflection

I could have spent more time focusing on creating a beautiful finish or high quality print, but I realized throughout this process that what I love most is the process of tinkering, recording, and tinkering until I get the right result. While it would be nice for these to be smooth and beautiful, I truly don’t care! I had way more fun creating an incredible spaghetti pile in grasshopper and fine-tuning my magnet system than I would have had sanding plastic.

Interesting things I learned

  • To create hollow areas in Rhino, put the items in place, run nonmanifoldmerge, then run createregions on the resulting object and delete the remaining input.
  • You can create support blockers in Cura! I had to block support to get truly hollow regions for magnets, and once I figured that out it was all smooth sailing.

    • On the left sidebar, at the bottom, you can turn on a support blocker. You have to apply it, then turn off support, then turn it back on, then reslice to get it to work.

  • I spent a lot of time adjusting the bed leveling knobs mid-print, and I found that worked really well for me. I don’t know why, but micro-adjustments every print really felt like the key to success.
  • These mangets are not that strong, but they are stronger when you double them up!
